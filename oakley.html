Oakley

Redesign of the website

Project in SPD Milan - User Experience (E-commerce)

Web design Program

Group project by Drake Derniowski - Frederick Bren d'Amour - Jan Riha and Trevina Daoud

Oakley designs, develops and manufactures sports performance equipment and lifestyle pieces. The goal of this project is to redesign part of www.oakley.com
Online surveys show that the website suffers particularly on:

1- Navigation
2- Product Browsing and Search/Filters
3- Product Information

Users find a lot of difficulties while searching and purchasing the product. They find problems in understanding the catalog hierarchy and the product they are looking for. We started to do our own resarch, define clients and user testing. We termined task flows and designed the wireframes to make the user experience as efficient as possible. At the end comes the UI, we designed specific pages for desktop and then the mobile version. For the responsive part: the main challenge is to maintain visual consistency and simplify the experience.

Analysis

After analysing, we took 3 important takeaways:

Product Titles are confusing: high level of difficulty in assessing the correct product.
Lack of ability to directly compare products, multiple tiles and seemingly repeating products with minor variations.
Lack of ability in differentiating or understanding different types of lens technologies.

Our Approach:

EDUCATING THROUGH SIMPLIFICATION, we applied our solutions to the main components of the Oakley website in terms of purchasing products. This included: Navigation, Product Listing Page, Product Detail Page. Improve PDP to simplify purchases and educate about lens techs, add oakley vision tool...

Overall Goal

Improve Understanding of Lens Tech
Simplify Product Listing Pages
Improve Product Knowlege
